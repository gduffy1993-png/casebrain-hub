# MUST_NOT_SAY_REGISTER.md

These phrases must be blocked or softened where material is missing, only mentioned, partial, or tracker-marked as a trap.

| Unsafe phrase | Why unsafe | Safe alternative |
|---|---|---|
| CCTV proves the allegation | CCTV is mentioned but may not be served or may be partial. | CCTV is said to exist; chase service/source footage before relying on it. |
| MG11 is fully served | Index or witness list may not equal served statement pages. | The bundle appears to refer to MG11 evidence; confirm the actual served statements. |
| CAD/999 supports the Crown sequence | CAD/999 may be requested or absent. | CAD/999 should be chased if timing is material and not served. |
| BWV confirms identification | BWV may be listed as outstanding or not listed. | BWV may be relevant; check MG6/service before relying on it. |
| Admission confirms guilt | Interview summaries can be partial, ambiguous or no-comment. | Interview material may affect position; review transcript/recording before characterising it. |
| Crown case is proven | CaseBrain must not determine guilt/proof. | The papers currently support the Crown allegation to this extent, subject to served material. |
| Medical evidence confirms injury severity | Medical may be triage note only or missing. | Medical evidence should be reviewed/chased before fixing injury severity. |
| Complainant is consistent | Only one short statement may be served. | The complainant account is recorded; consistency needs comparison across material. |
| Unused material has been disclosed | MG6 may be absent or decision may say inspect/withhold/outstanding. | Unused material position is unclear; chase MG6/MG6C and disclosure decision. |
| Listing date is 24 August | Old MG5 date may conflict with later notice. | Latest listing notice should control next hearing date. |
| The defendant has no defence | Software must not make legal advice or final outcome claims. | Current papers create pressure points and risks; take instructions. |
| Phone download shows supply | Phone may be seized but download absent. | Phone material is referred to; chase download/extraction if relied upon. |
| Drug weight proves PWITS | Weight alone may not prove intent; lab/packaging/phone/cash may be missing. | PWITS inference depends on served quantity, packaging, phone/cash and expert context. |
| Custody interview transcript is served | Summary may exist without transcript. | Interview summary served; chase full transcript/recording if material. |
| Co-defendant account proves client involvement | Co-defendant may be untested or not served. | Co-defendant material is a risk/issue; avoid treating it as proof without served statement/interview. |
| Forensic result matches defendant | Forensic exhibit may be referenced but report absent. | Forensic material should be chased before relying on match/provenance. |
| The court will accept this argument | Prediction/assurance is unsafe. | A solicitor could raise this issue conditionally, tied to the served papers. |
| No disclosure issues | Absence of schedule may hide issues, not eliminate them. | No clear disclosure issue is apparent from served papers; check schedules and outstanding items. |

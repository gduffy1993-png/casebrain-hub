Summary of the case

1. The prosecution case is that on 2 April 2024 the defendant Ashleigh Merritt entered ValueMart, 45 Retail Park, Northshire, and placed items of clothing and cosmetics into a bag she had brought with her. She left the store without paying. The total value of the goods was £247.50. She was stopped by store security in the car park. The goods were recovered. The defendant was arrested and taken to Northshire Police Station.

2. The prosecution say the defendant dishonestly appropriated property belonging to ValueMart with the intention of permanently depriving the store of it. Store CCTV covers the aisles and the exit. The incident was captured on CCTV. CCTV from the store has been requested; footage from 11:00 to 12:30 on the date in question has been secured. A customer who was in the queue, Mr Owen Finch, saw the defendant leave in a hurry and mentioned it to staff. Emergency services were not called from the store; security called the police on the non-emergency number. The call recording and CAD log are retained by the force.

3. The defendant was interviewed on 2 April 2024. She gave a prepared statement denying theft and stating that she had intended to pay but had become confused. She did not answer further questions. Her custody record and interview record form part of the disclosure. Body worn video was used by the attending officer when the defendant was arrested in the car park; the BWV is retained. Disclosure of the full CCTV window and any continuity documentation will be pursued.

4. The prosecution will rely on the evidence of Dean Hooper (store security), Lisa Treadwell (store manager), the store CCTV when served, the defendant's interview and custody records, and the recovered goods. The prosecution case is that the defendant acted dishonestly and intended to permanently deprive ValueMart of the goods.

MG11 — WITNESS STATEMENT
================================================================================

Statement of: Dean HOOPER
Age: 29 (date of birth: 7 July 1994)
Address: 15 Station Road, Northshire, NSh5 3RT
Occupation: Security officer, ValueMart Northshire

This statement consisting of 2 pages is true to the best of my knowledge and belief. I make it knowing that it may be used in court.

Signed: Dean Hooper
Date: 3 April 2024

Statement

1. I work as a security officer at ValueMart, 45 Retail Park, Northshire. On 2 April 2024 I was on duty. I was monitoring the store via CCTV and doing floor walks. At approximately 11:40 I saw a woman in the clothing section placing items into a large bag she had with her. She did not use a basket. She was not scanning or paying for the items. I kept observation.

2. She went through the cosmetics aisle and added more items to the bag. She then walked towards the exit. She did not go to any till. She left the store. I followed and stopped her in the car park. I identified myself and asked her to return to the store. She was reluctant but came back. The bag was opened and the items were identified as store stock. The total value was £247.50. The store manager Lisa Treadwell was called. The police were called. I did not make the call myself; Ms Treadwell did. I understand the call was recorded.

3. The incident took place between about 11:35 and 11:50. The store CCTV would have captured the defendant in the aisles and at the exit. I am willing to give evidence in court. I have read this statement and it is true.

MG11 — WITNESS STATEMENT
================================================================================

Statement of: Lisa TREADWELL
Age: 41 (date of birth: 19 March 1983)
Address: 4 Meadow Close, Northshire, NSh6 2KL
Occupation: Store manager, ValueMart Northshire

This statement consisting of 2 pages is true to the best of my knowledge and belief. I make it knowing that it may be used in court.

Signed: Lisa Treadwell
Date: 4 April 2024

Statement

1. I am the store manager at ValueMart, 45 Retail Park, Northshire. On 2 April 2024 I was in the office when security, Dean Hooper, informed me that he had stopped a female in the car park with unpaid goods. I went down and was present when the items were checked. The goods were all from our store. Value £247.50. The female gave her name as Ashleigh Merritt. She said she had meant to pay but got confused. I did not see the actual taking; I was told about it by Dean and I have seen the CCTV since. The CCTV clearly shows her putting items in the bag and leaving without paying.

2. A customer had mentioned to one of our staff that someone had left in a hurry. I believe that customer was Owen Finch. He did not give a full statement at the time but said he would be willing to be contacted. Our CCTV covers the tills and the main aisles. We keep footage for 31 days. The police have requested a copy. I have passed the request to head office. The footage for 11:00 to 12:30 on 2 April has been requested.

3. I called the police on the store phone. I used the non-emergency number. The call was logged. I am willing to give evidence in court. I have read this statement and it is true.

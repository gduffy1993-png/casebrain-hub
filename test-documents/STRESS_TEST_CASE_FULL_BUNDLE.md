## Full summary of case

At approximately 22:45 on 14 September 2024, the complainant Lewis James HART was in the rear car park of The Crown public house, 18 High Street, Manchester. He had been inside the premises with friends and had gone to the car park to retrieve an item from his vehicle. The defendant Jordan Michael WRIGHT was also at the premises with two associates. The defendant and complainant were not known to each other. There was no prior contact between them inside the pub.

The defendant approached the complainant in the car park. Witnesses describe an exchange of words; the complainant was walking back towards the pub entrance when the defendant came up behind him, took hold of his shoulder and turned him round. The complainant raised his hands in a non-aggressive manner and stated that he did not want any trouble. The defendant then struck the complainant once to the face with a closed fist. The complainant fell backwards and struck the back of his head on the tarmac surface of the car park. He did not get up. The defendant did not strike again. Members of the public and staff came to the complainant’s assistance. The emergency services were called. The defendant remained at the scene and was detained by police on arrival.

The complainant was taken by ambulance to Manchester Royal Infirmary. He was found to have facial bruising and swelling, a laceration to the occipital region requiring suturing, and minor abrasions consistent with a fall. Imaging confirmed no skull fracture or intracranial injury. He was discharged the same night with follow-up instructions.

The prosecution case is that the defendant intentionally caused grievous bodily harm. The single punch caused the complainant to fall and sustain a significant head injury. The prosecution will rely on the fact that the defendant initiated the confrontation, that the complainant offered no violence, and that the blow was delivered without lawful excuse. The issue of intent (s.18 versus s.20) may be live; the medical evidence does not indicate a sustained or repeated assault but rather a single blow and fall. Defence have indicated a possible basis of self-defence; no formal defence statement has been served at the date of this summary. The defendant made no comment in interview after legal advice.

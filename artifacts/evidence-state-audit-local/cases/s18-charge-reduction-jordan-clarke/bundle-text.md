**Fight Charge (Trial Strategy):** Challenge identification reliability under Turnbull guidelines. If identification is unreliable or excluded, case collapses. Target: identification challenge leading to acquittal or dismissal.

### Fallback Objective
**Charge Reduction (s18 → s20):** If identification is admitted, require prosecution to articulate intent theory early and aim for charge reduction from s18 to s20 or lesser offence. Defence accepts harm occurred but challenges that intent meets s18 threshold. Target: proportional downgrade under case management, not a concession.

### 1. Identification (Turnbull Issues)
- **Weakness:** Poor lighting, partial angles, hoods obscuring faces
- **Evidence:** CCTV stills show limited facial visibility; witnesses express uncertainty
- **Pressure:** Request Turnbull direction; challenge identification reliability

- **Evidence:** Injury pattern supports brief incident rather than sustained attack
- **Pressure:** Medical evidence does not support s18 intent threshold
- **Action:** Request medical expert clarification on mechanism and intent implications

### 3. Incident Duration/Sequence
- **Weakness:** Incident lasted 15-30 seconds; chaotic, not prolonged
- **Evidence:** CCTV and witness statements support brief scuffle
- **Pressure:** Brief duration undermines sustained/targeted conduct required for s18
- **Action:** Emphasise sequence/duration evidence in submissions

### 4. Forensic Limitations
- **Weakness:** Partial DNA profile, mixed contributors, not exclusive
- **Evidence:** Forensic report states secondary transfer possible
- **Pressure:** Forensic evidence does not definitively link defendant to weapon use
- **Action:** Request full forensic methodology disclosure; challenge exclusivity

### 5. Disclosure Gaps
- **Weakness:** MG6C incomplete; CCTV continuity gaps; phone data pending
- **Evidence:** Disclosure correspondence shows outstanding items
- **Pressure:** Incomplete disclosure undermines trial readiness
- **Action:** Chase disclosure with timetable; seek directions if not complete

- **Weakness:** CCTV and weapon continuity gaps documented
- **Evidence:** Exhibit log shows gaps between recovery/download and formal logging
- **Pressure:** Continuity gaps may affect admissibility/weight
- **Action:** Request full continuity documentation; clarify gaps

## Win Conditions (Fight Charge — Primary)
1. Identification successfully challenged/excluded under Turnbull; prosecution case collapses
2. OR identification admitted but unreliable; court applies Turnbull warnings; identification heavily qualified
3. OR identification admitted but intent not proven beyond reasonable doubt; medical supports single/brief blow; CCTV shows no sustained/targeted conduct; weapon use lacks targeting/duration

## Win Conditions (Charge Reduction — Fallback)
1. If identification admitted: prosecution cannot articulate s18 intent beyond reasonable doubt
2. Medical evidence shows mechanism consistent with single/brief blow (not sustained/targeted)
3. CCTV/sequence evidence shows no prolonged or targeted conduct
4. Weapon use lacks duration/targeting to prove specific intent
5. Court accepts proportional downgrade (s18 → s20) under case management

## Loss Conditions
1. Identification admitted and found reliable; prosecution case proceeds
2. Prosecution maintains s18 and evidences specific intent with sustained/targeted conduct
3. Medical evidence shows injuries consistent with repeated/targeted blows
4. CCTV/sequence evidence shows prolonged or deliberate attack
5. Weapon use demonstrates targeting/duration supporting intent
6. Court rejects identification challenge and declines proportional downgrade

## Priority Evidence (Fight Charge)
1. **Identification conditions:** Lighting, distance, duration, obstruction (hood), witness uncertainty, CCTV quality, similar clothing
2. **CCTV quality assessment:** Facial identification reliability, visibility, angle limitations
3. **Witness identification:** Viewing conditions, certainty levels, first accounts

## Priority Evidence (Charge Reduction Fallback)
1. Medical evidence: injury severity (non-determinative) and mechanism (determinative for intent)
2. Sequence/duration evidence from CCTV or witnesses (brief vs sustained conduct)
3. Weapon recovery and manner of use (duration, targeting, escalation)
4. Defendant's actions before and after incident (planning vs reactive)

## Procedural Actions
1. **Request Turnbull direction** on identification reliability
2. **Challenge CCTV quality** for identification purposes; request quality assessment
3. Chase outstanding disclosure with clear timetable as proportional case management and record responses
4. Document missing items and seek directions if timetable slips (stay/abuse only if failures persist after clear chase trail and directions/timetable)
5. Request full CCTV export with continuity from source device to exhibit
6. Request completed MG6C/unused schedule; note missing/late items
7. Request forensic methodology and chain of custody for weapon; clarify handling gap
8. Request medical mechanism clarification (single vs sustained action)

## Issues to be Tried

### 1. Identification (Primary Issue)
The defence will challenge the reliability of identification evidence under Turnbull guidelines. The identification conditions were poor:
- **Lighting:** Variable; streetlights and takeaway window lighting; areas of shadow; not optimal for facial identification
- **Distance:** Witnesses at 3–50 metres; complainant at close range but brief exposure (~15–30 seconds)
- **Duration:** Incident visible for 15–30 seconds; identification window brief
- **Obstruction:** Alleged attacker wore dark hooded top with hood up throughout; facial features not clearly visible in any CCTV still or witness account
- **Angle:** CCTV angles partial; signage and camera positioning limit facial visibility; back/side views primarily
- **Similarity:** Multiple individuals in area wearing similar dark clothing; no distinctive features reliably distinguishing attacker from others
- **CCTV Quality:** CCTV footage is unreliable for positive identification purposes; facial features not clearly visible; identification would require inference from clothing and general build, which is insufficient for Turnbull compliance
- **Witness Uncertainty:** All witnesses express uncertainty; CCTV stills show similarity but not certainty

The defence will invite the court to apply Turnbull guidelines and find that identification is not reliable. If identification is unreliable or excluded, the case fails. If identification is admitted but heavily qualified, the defence will proceed to challenge intent (see Issue 2).

### 2. Intent (s18 vs s20) — Fallback Issue
**If identification is admitted:** The defence accepts that harm occurred but contests that the intent threshold for s18 is met. The defence case is:
- The incident was brief (15-30 seconds) and chaotic
- The medical evidence shows a mechanism consistent with a single motion, not sustained/targeted conduct
- There is no evidence of planning or premeditation
- The weapon use lacks the duration/targeting required to prove specific intent
- The circumstances are consistent with s20 (unlawful wounding) rather than s18 (wounding with intent)

### 3. Sequence/Duration
The defence will invite the court to find that the incident was brief and chaotic, not prolonged or targeted. The evidence shows:
- Incident duration: 15-30 seconds
- No evidence of sustained attack
- No evidence of targeting or escalation
- Medical mechanism supports single motion

### 4. Forensic Evidence
The defence will challenge the weight of forensic evidence:
- DNA profile is partial and mixed (multiple contributors)
- Profile is not exclusive to defendant
- Secondary transfer cannot be ruled out
- Continuity gaps may affect admissibility/weight

### 5. Disclosure
The defence will seek clarification on:
- Incomplete MG6C schedule
- CCTV continuity gaps
- Phone data reliance (if any)
- Forensic methodology and chain of custody

## Defence Position
The defence does not accept the s18 charge. The defence position is that:
1. **Primary:** Identification is unreliable under Turnbull and should be excluded or heavily qualified. If identification fails, case collapses.
2. **Fallback:** If identification is admitted, intent does not meet s18 threshold
3. If identification is admitted, the circumstances support s20 (unlawful wounding) or alternative lesser offence
4. Proportional charge reduction should be considered under case management if identification is admitted

The defence will invite the court to apply Turnbull guidelines on identification. If identification is admitted, the defence will invite the prosecution to review the charge and consider reduction to s20 based on the evidence available.

## Introduction
This skeleton sets out the defence position on identification reliability (Turnbull challenge) and, if identification is admitted, charge reduction from s18 OAPA 1861 (wounding with intent) to s20 OAPA 1861 (unlawful wounding) or alternative lesser offence.

## Legal Framework
- **s18 OAPA 1861:** Requires specific intent to cause grievous bodily harm
- **s20 OAPA 1861:** Unlawful wounding without specific intent requirement
- **Case Law:** Intent must be proved beyond reasonable doubt; brief/chaotic incidents may not support s18

## Defence Submissions

### 1. Intent Threshold Not Met
The prosecution must prove specific intent to cause GBH. The evidence does not support this:
- Incident was brief (15-30 seconds) and chaotic
- No evidence of planning or premeditation
- No evidence of sustained or targeted conduct
- Medical mechanism consistent with single motion, not sustained attack

### 3. Medical Evidence (Fallback — if identification admitted)
The medical report states:
- Injury mechanism consistent with single sharp force trauma
- Mechanism could be consistent with either deliberate single strike or single motion during brief/chaotic altercation
- Injury does not show evidence of multiple strikes or sustained attack
- Intent cannot be determined from medical evidence alone

The medical evidence does not support the s18 intent threshold.

### 3. Sequence/Duration Evidence
- CCTV and witness evidence shows incident lasting 15-30 seconds
- No evidence of prolonged or targeted conduct
- Circumstances support brief/chaotic altercation rather than sustained attack
- Sequence does not support specific intent inference

### 5. Weapon Use (Fallback — if identification admitted)
- Weapon recovered nearby with partial DNA (mixed profile, not exclusive)
- No evidence of weapon use demonstrating targeting/duration
- Weapon presence alone does not establish s18 intent
- Weight depends on manner, duration, targeting - all absent here

## Proportional Case Management
**Primary route:** Identification challenge under Turnbull. If identification is excluded or heavily qualified, case fails.

**Fallback route (if identification admitted):** The defence invites the court to consider:
- Proportional charge reduction under case management
- s18 → s20 reduction reflects the evidence available
- Reduction is not a concession but reflects intent contestability
- Early charge review promotes efficient case management

## Conclusion
The defence submits that:
1. **Primary:** Identification is unreliable under Turnbull and should be excluded or heavily qualified. If identification fails, case collapses.
2. **Fallback (if identification admitted):** The evidence does not support s18 intent threshold
3. **Fallback (if identification admitted):** The circumstances support s20 (unlawful wounding) or alternative lesser offence
4. **Fallback (if identification admitted):** Proportional charge reduction should be considered

The defence invites the court to apply Turnbull guidelines on identification. If identification is admitted, the defence invites the prosecution to consider charge reduction and, if not agreed, will seek a ruling from the court.

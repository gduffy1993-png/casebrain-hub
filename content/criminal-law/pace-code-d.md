# PACE Code D – Identification
Summary reference for identification procedures (England & Wales). See legislation.gov.uk for full text.

---

## Application of Code D
Code D applies when a police officer has reasonable grounds to suspect the involvement of a person in a criminal offence and the person is at a police station or otherwise in detention. It governs identification procedures: parade (line-up), group identification, video identification, confrontation, and the taking of fingerprints, footwear impressions, and samples.

---

## Identification by witnesses
Where the identity of the suspect is known to the police and the suspect is available, the identification officer may use a video identification, an identification parade, or a group identification. Where the suspect is not known, the identification officer may show witnesses photographs or other images (e.g. E-FIT) in accordance with Annex E. Video identification is the preferred method. The suspect must be given written notice and the opportunity to have a solicitor or friend present. The suspect may refuse to take part; an identification may still be held with the suspect’s consent using a photograph or image.

---

## Identification parades
An identification parade must be held where the suspect disputes identification and the identification officer considers it practicable. At least eight persons (in addition to the suspect) who resemble the suspect in age, height, general appearance and position in life must be used. The suspect may select his or her own position. Each witness must be asked whether they can identify the person they saw on a previous occasion; they must not be prompted. The procedure must be recorded.

---

## Video identification
Video identification is the preferred method when the suspect is known and available. A moving or still image of the suspect is shown together with images of at least eight other people who resemble the suspect. The images must be shown in a random order. The witness must be asked whether they can identify the person they saw. The suspect’s solicitor or friend may be present. The procedure must be recorded.

---

## Group identification
Where it is more practicable than a parade or video identification, the identification officer may arrange for the witness to see the suspect in an informal group (e.g. in a public place). The suspect’s consent is required unless it is impracticable to hold a parade or video identification. The suspect may have a solicitor or friend present. The procedure must be recorded.

---

## Confrontation
Confrontation (the witness is brought face to face with the suspect) may be used only when no parade or video or group identification is practicable. The suspect must be told of the right to free legal advice. The procedure must be recorded.

---

## Fingerprints and samples
Fingerprints and footwear impressions may be taken from a person detained at a police station with consent or without consent where authorised (e.g. for a recordable offence). Non-intimate samples (e.g. saliva, hair other than pubic) may be taken with consent or without consent where authorised. Intimate samples (e.g. blood, urine, dental impression) may be taken only with consent and only by a registered medical practitioner or nurse. Reasonable force may be used where authorised to take fingerprints, footwear impressions or non-intimate samples. Retention and destruction of biometric data are governed by the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012.

---

## Exclusion of evidence
Failure to comply with Code D may lead to evidence of identification being excluded under section 78 of PACE 1984 if the court considers that the admission of the evidence would have such an adverse effect on the fairness of the proceedings that it ought not to be admitted.

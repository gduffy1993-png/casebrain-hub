# PACE Codes A, B, C, E – Summary reference
Summary of Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 Codes (England & Wales). See gov.uk and legislation.gov.uk for full text.

---

## Code A – Stop and search
Code A governs the exercise of stop and search powers. An officer may stop and search a person or vehicle where he or she has reasonable grounds to suspect that stolen or prohibited articles (or certain other items) will be found. The officer must give the person searched his or her name and station, the object of the search, the legal power used, and that a record will be made. The person is entitled to a copy of the record. Searches must be conducted with regard to the dignity of the person; strip search may only be conducted in accordance with Annex A. Covert surveillance and retention of material are subject to separate legislation (RIPA, IPA).

---

## Code B – Search of premises and seizure
Code B applies to searches of premises by police (with or without a warrant) and the seizure of property. Entry may be by consent or under warrant or other statutory power. Before searching, the officer must state the purpose and legal power and give notice of the right to a copy of the search record. Premises may be searched only to the extent necessary. Items subject to legal privilege, excluded material and special procedure material have enhanced protection; seizure is subject to PACE and the Criminal Justice and Police Act 2001. Reasonable force may be used where necessary.

---

## Code C – Detention, treatment and questioning
Code C governs the detention, treatment and questioning of persons in police custody. A person arrested and taken to a police station must be told of the right to free legal advice and the right to have someone informed. The custody officer must open a custody record and ensure the detainee is treated in accordance with the Code. Reviews of detention must be carried out by the review officer at prescribed intervals (generally within 6 hours of charge or first review, then at 9-hour intervals). The maximum period of detention without charge is generally 24 hours (extendable to 36 hours by superintendent, and beyond by magistrates up to 96 hours for indictable offences). Juveniles and vulnerable persons have additional safeguards. Interviews must be conducted in accordance with the Code; the right to silence and the adverse inference provisions (Criminal Justice and Public Order Act 1994) must be explained when applicable. Breach may lead to exclusion of evidence under section 78 PACE.

---

## Code C – Right to legal advice
A detainee must be informed clearly of the right to free legal advice and to speak to a solicitor in private. The right may be exercised at any time. Delay is permitted only in connection with certain serious offences and only on authority of an officer of rank specified in the Code; the detainee must be told the reason for delay. Where the detainee has asked for legal advice, the interview may not begin (or continue) until the solicitor is present unless certain exceptions apply (e.g. superintendent authorisation in urgent cases, or the detainee has waived the right in writing).

---

## Code E – Audio recording of interviews
Code E applies to the audio recording of interviews of suspects at police stations. Interviews in respect of indictable offences (including either-way) must be audio recorded, subject to limited exceptions. The recording must be carried out in the presence of the suspect. The suspect must be told that the interview is being recorded and given a notice about the use of the recording. At the start of the interview the officer must state the offence, the date and time, and the identity of those present. A master copy must be sealed and a working copy used for subsequent use. Failure to comply may result in exclusion of the interview under section 78 PACE.
